Part G: Linux Security Research
1. Why are file permissions important?

File permissions control who can read, modify, and execute files. They help protect sensitive information and maintain system security.

2. What happens if sensitive files are given 777 permissions?

Any user can read, modify, or delete the file. This can lead to data theft, unauthorized changes, and security breaches.

3. What is the Principle of Least Privilege?

The Principle of Least Privilege means users should only receive the minimum permissions necessary to perform their tasks.

Benefits:

Better security
Reduced risk
Prevention of accidental damage
4. Why do organizations restrict user access?

Organizations restrict access to:

Protect sensitive data
Prevent unauthorized actions
Reduce insider threats
Meet compliance requirements
Improve overall security
